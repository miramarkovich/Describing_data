The Total score of Each University Varies by Population

The correlation coefficient for the number of students was .076, implying a positive relationship and the lowest theoretical strength of the variables. The correlation coefficient for the student-staff ratio was -0.21, which means a negative correlation and higher theoretical strength. The correlation coefficient for the percent of international students was .27, which has a positive relationship with the highest theoretical strength of the variables. The correlation coefficient for the percent of female students was -0.127, meaning a negative correlation and lower theoretical strength. The correlation coefficient is similar to the scatterplot since when there is a positive correlation coefficient, the data looks like it is increasing overall, and when there is a negative correlation, there is an overall decrease in the data. Although correlation coefficients can be a great tool for looking at trends in data, things like outliers and the sample size can impact the correlation coefficient.
